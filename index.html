The problem

Getting honest feedback on your idea is harder than building it

You have a business idea. Maybe you've already started. You need honest, outside perspective before you commit more time, money, or energy. So what do you do?

  • ✕ Post on Reddit — get three responses, two of which are "cool idea bro"
  • ✕ Ask friends and family — they love you too much to tell you the truth
  • ✕ Hire a mentor — $300–500/hr for someone who may not understand your specific situation
  • ✕ Post on LinkedIn — hope someone smart notices. Feedback is public, random, and performative

None of these give you what you actually need: structured, honest critique from a peer who is equally invested in the process — because they need the same thing back.

How it works

Give a great critique. Get a great critique.

FounderCrit borrows the most powerful mechanic from writing critique communities — mutual obligation — and applies it to entrepreneurship.

01
Submit your profile

Tell us what you're building, who it's for, what stage you're at, and what kind of feedback you need.

02
Get matched 1-on-1

We pair you with a peer based on stage, industry, and feedback focus. A partner, not a forum.

03
Give your critique first

Complete a structured, rubric-guided critique of your partner's idea. The rubric is what makes feedback actionable.

04
Receive yours simultaneously

Once both critiques are complete, feedback is exchanged at the same time. Nobody sees theirs until they've given.

The feedback rubric

Structured feedback that actually moves you forward

Every critique follows the same five-part rubric. No vague opinions. No random observations. Just specific, actionable feedback across the dimensions that matter most at your stage.

Viability

Is there a real market? Can this make money? Who would actually pay for this?

Clarity

Is the idea clearly defined? Could a stranger understand it in 60 seconds?

Blind spots

What risks or obstacles might you be missing? What's the honest concern?

Resonance

Does the brand, message, or positioning land? Is the story compelling?

Top 3 actions

Concrete next steps your reviewer would take if this were their idea. Specific, prioritized, honest.
